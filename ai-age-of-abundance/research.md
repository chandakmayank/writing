# Research: AI, Age of Abundance, and Universal High Income

## Core Thesis
AI and robotics are creating near-zero marginal cost production, potentially transitioning humanity from scarcity economics to abundance economics. The challenge shifts from production to distribution.

---

## Key Voices & Their Positions

### Sam Altman (OpenAI CEO)
- Coined "universal high income" at SXSW London (July 2025): "We may need things like universal high income, or some way of distributing all the additional productivity that AI will produce in the economy" [^1]
- Called AI "massively deflationary" — will make money worth vastly more [^2]
- Wants "universal extreme wealth" not just basic income — argued traditional UBI would leave people wanting [^3]
- Predicted $1,000 of AI inference could replace a whole team's software work by end of 2025 [^2]

### Dario Amodei (Anthropic CEO)
- Essay "Machines of Loving Grace" (Oct 2024): Most people underestimate both AI's upside AND its risks [^4]
- Key framework: "marginal returns to intelligence" — intelligence is becoming the dominant factor of production [^4]
- Predicts "country of geniuses in a datacenter" — millions of AI instances running 10-100x human speed [^4]
- Five areas of transformation: biology/health, neuroscience/mental health, economic development/poverty, peace/governance, work/meaning [^4]
- Timeline: powerful AI could arrive as early as 2026 [^4]

### Peter Diamandis (XPRIZE, Singularity University)
- MOSAIC Model for Universal High Income funding: [^5]
  1. Dynamic VAT capturing AI-driven cost reductions
  2. Ring-fence above-trend tax receipts from AI-boosted profits
  3. Savings from automating government itself
  4. Consolidate fragmented welfare into negative income tax

### Eric Schmidt (Former Google CEO)
- Says AI is "under-hyped" and its biggest gains are still ahead [^6]
- TED Talk (2025): "The arrival of non-human intelligence is a very big deal" [^7]

### Yanis Varoufakis (Economist)
- Warns of "techno-feudalism" — value flows as rent to those who own digital "land" (clouds, platforms, models) [^8]
- Profit drove capitalism, rent drove feudalism — now we have "cloud capital, algorithmic capital" [^8]

## Economic Impact Data

### GDP & Productivity Forecasts
- **Goldman Sachs**: Generative AI could raise global GDP by 7% (~$7 trillion) and lift productivity growth by 1.5 percentage points over 10 years [^9]
- **PwC (April 2025)**: AI could boost global economic output by up to 15 percentage points over the next decade — effectively adding one percentage point to annual growth rates [^10]
- **McKinsey**: Generative AI alone could boost annual productivity growth by 0.1 to 0.6 percentage points through 2040; combined with automation: 0.5 to 3.4 percentage points [^11]
- **MIT (Daron Acemoglu)**: More conservative — AI will add just 1% GDP growth over 10 years [^12]
- **Goldman Sachs**: AI may start to measurably boost US GDP in 2027 [^13]

### Inference Cost Decline
- **Epoch AI**: LLM inference prices falling dramatically — 9x to 900x per year depending on benchmark [^14]
- GPT-4 performance on PhD-level science questions: price fell 40x per year [^14]
- Fastest drops occurred in the past year (2025) [^14]

### Developer Productivity
- 84% of developers use AI tools, 41% of all code is AI-generated (2026) [^15]
- Productivity gains of 25-39% when using AI tools [^15]
- AI coding tools cut time to complete programming tasks by 50% in controlled trials [^16]
- Customer support AI assistants raised productivity by 14% [^16]

### Labor Market Impact
- Goldman Sachs: 300 million full-time jobs exposed to automation [^9]
- Two-thirds of US occupations exposed to some degree of AI automation [^9]
- Of exposed occupations, 25-50% of workload could be replaced [^9]
- 60% of today's workers are employed in occupations that didn't exist in 1940 [^9]

## The Deflation Thesis

### Sam Altman's Deflation Argument
- "Given progress with work you can do in front of a computer, but also what looks like it will soon happen with robotics... we're going to have massively deflationary pressure" [^2]
- Things will get "radically cheaper" and "empowerment of individual people" will go up [^2]
- This would be an inversion of virtually every economic system in history [^2]

### How AI Drives Deflation
1. **Productivity boom**: More output per worker → lower unit costs → competitive pressure to lower prices
2. **Automation**: Reduces labor costs across manufacturing, services, knowledge work
3. **Supply chain optimization**: AI-driven logistics and inventory management
4. **Energy efficiency**: AI-optimized grids, materials, processes
5. **Knowledge work cost collapse**: Legal, medical, financial advice becomes near-free

### Historical Parallel: The Internet
- Internet reduced information distribution costs to near-zero
- AI could reduce cognitive labor costs to near-zero
- Both create "good deflation" — falling prices WITH growth

## Critiques & Counterarguments

### The Wage Compression Problem
- Economist Dean Baker: Labor compensation as share of consumption dipped below 72% (from 75-76% pre-pandemic) — represents ~$1 trillion in annual consumption without wage growth [^17]
- That $1 trillion is "fueled by inflated stock portfolios, venture capital gambles, and data center construction" [^17]
- Baker argues the AI bubble bursting would actually be good for working class Americans [^17]

### Techno-Feudalism Risk
- Varoufakis: If AI is deployed inside concentrated, lock-in heavy platforms, productivity gains show up as higher rents, more subscriptions, more tolls — not falling prices [^5][^8]
- "Enshittification" (Cory Doctorow): Platforms serve users well → shift value to business customers → extract for shareholders [^5]

### Productivity Paradox
- Surveys show number of people using AI at work is falling [^2]
- Many workers argue AI is "essentially useless to them" despite employer pressure [^2]
- MIT economist Acemoglu: AI will add just 1% GDP — far less than optimists predict [^12]

### Energy & Infrastructure Constraints
- OpenAI's data centers require direct access to nuclear power and massive solar farms [^18]
- National grid cannot handle AI compute demand [^18]
- OpenAI loses $3 for every $1 it earns [^18]

## Universal Basic Income vs Universal High Income

### UBI Pilots
- Welsh government: 600 young care leavers receiving £1,600/month for 18 months [^19]
- Sam Altman funded UBI pilot in Oakland (2016-2019): $1,000/month to 1,000 people [^20]

### Universal High Income (UHI) — Key Distinction
- UBI: Minimum floor to survive
- UHI: Abundance-level income enabled by AI productivity [^5]
- Altman argues traditional UBI would "leave people wanting" — prefers "universal extreme wealth" [^3]
- Mechanism: AI makes goods/services so cheap that even modest cash transfers provide high living standards

### MOSAIC Model (Diamandis/Schreiber)
1. Dynamic VAT on AI-driven cost reductions
2. Ring-fence AI-boosted tax receipts
3. Automate government → redirect savings
4. Consolidate welfare → negative income tax
5. Public "AI dividend dashboard" for transparency [^5]

## Sectors Where AI Creates Abundance

### Healthcare
- Amodei: AI could compress 50-100 years of biological progress into 5-10 years [^4]
- Drug discovery: AI could design and test drugs orders of magnitude faster
- Diagnostics: AI matching or exceeding radiologist accuracy
- Personalized medicine: Every person gets custom treatment plans

### Education
- AI tutors could provide 1-on-1 expert instruction to every human
- Cost of education could drop dramatically
- Language barriers eliminated by AI translation

### Energy
- AI-optimized solar, nuclear fusion research acceleration
- Grid optimization reducing waste
- Materials science breakthroughs via AI simulation

### Legal & Financial Services
- Legal advice becoming near-free via AI
- Financial planning accessible to all income levels
- Tax optimization democratized

### Software & Knowledge Work
- 41% of code already AI-generated (2026) [^15]
- Cost of building software approaching zero
- Any person can build applications via AI coding assistants

## Timeline Estimates

### Near-term (2025-2027)
- AI coding tools become standard, 50%+ of new code AI-assisted
- White-collar productivity gains visible in GDP data
- Inference costs continue exponential decline
- AI companies reach profitability or face consolidation

### Medium-term (2027-2030)
- AI significantly impacts healthcare diagnostics and drug discovery
- Robotics integration in manufacturing accelerates
- Labor market displacement becomes visible in unemployment data
- Policy debates around UBI/UHI intensify

### Long-term (2030-2035)
- PwC: AI adds 15 percentage points to global GDP [^10]
- Deflationary pressure on goods and services becomes measurable
- New economic models (MOSAIC or alternatives) implemented
- "Age of Abundance" becomes visible in living standards data
