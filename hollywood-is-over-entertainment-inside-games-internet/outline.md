# Outline: Hollywood Is Over — The Entertainment Business Is Inside Video Games and Internet Mediums

## 1. Hook
- 2026 Oscars: NOT A SINGLE Best Picture nominee filmed on a Hollywood soundstage
- "The sunny version of Detroit" — former Sony Pictures CEO on LA
- 41,000 workers fled Hollywood 2022-2024
- Meanwhile: 3.5 billion people play video games, $10B creator economy in gaming alone

## 2. Introduction
- What's happening: Hollywood is structurally declining while entertainment moves to games and internet platforms
- Why it matters: Entertainment is America's #1 soft power export — and it's leaving traditional institutions
- Who this affects: Creators, investors, anyone in media, anyone who consumes entertainment (everyone)

## 3. The Death of Hollywood's Cluster
- Hard numbers: LA shoot days -46% (2022-2025), box office -40% inflation-adjusted
- The cluster effect failing: talent leaving, studios selling, infrastructure hollowing out
- 2023 strikes accelerated the collapse
- Most powerful entertainment person: Netflix's Ted Sarandos (Silicon Valley, not LA)
- Studios fighting over scraps: Warner Bros Discovery sold, $6B+ in "synergies" coming (layoffs)
- AI as the final threat

## 4. Gaming: The New Entertainment King
- Revenue scale: gaming > film + music combined
- Platform power: Twitch 31M daily viewers, Roblox 85M daily users, YouTube Gaming 100B watch hours
- Gaming as social space: Fortnite concerts (12M viewers), Roblox worlds, virtual events
- Creator economy: $10B generated in 2025, top streamers earning millions
- Live streaming: $62B market in 2026, TikTok Live surpassed Twitch in hours watched

## 5. Gen Z: The Gaming-First Generation
- 63% prefer games over films, 87% game weekly
- Gaming = 25% of leisure time (beats social media and streaming)
- Mobile-first: 86% identify as mobile gamers
- 6+ hours/day average gaming
- Social discovery over traditional media: TikTok, YouTube, Instagram — not TV/film critics

## 6. The Reverse IP Pipeline
- Gaming IP flowing TO Hollywood, not away from it
- Super Mario Movie: $1B+ box office
- The Last of Us: HBO tentpole
- Half of top 10 game movies released in last 5 years
- 50+ game movies in development
- Hollywood now depends on gaming for fresh IP — the old studios can't create new franchises that stick

## 7. Streaming and Internet Mediums Eating Traditional TV
- OTT replacing linear TV as default
- Live streaming explosion: Kick (95/5 splits), TikTok Live, YouTube dominance
- Creator economy: 50M+ independent creators, direct-to-audience monetization
- Non-endemic brands (insurance, airlines) moving ad budgets to gaming creators

## 8. What This Means
- Entertainment is now interactive, creator-driven, platform-distributed
- The studio system is becoming a legacy supplier of content to internet platforms
- Power shifting from institutions (studios, networks) to platforms (YouTube, Twitch, Roblox) and creators
- The future of "Hollywood" is wherever the audience actually spends time — and that's games

## 9. Getting Started / The New Reality
- For creators: gaming and internet platforms are where the money and audience are
- For studios: adapt or become content suppliers to platform overlords
- For viewers: you're already living in the new entertainment world — you just might not have noticed
- The old gatekeepers are gone. The audience decides.
