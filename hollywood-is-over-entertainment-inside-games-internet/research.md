# Research: Hollywood Is Over — The Entertainment Business Is Inside Video Games and Internet Mediums

## Overview

The traditional Hollywood entertainment industry is experiencing a structural decline while the entertainment business shifts to video games, streaming platforms, and internet-native mediums. This research covers the decline of Hollywood, the rise of gaming as entertainment, the creator economy, Gen Z consumption patterns, and the future of entertainment.

---

## 1. Hollywood's Decline: Hard Numbers

### Production Collapse in Los Angeles
- LA shoot days plunged from 36,792 (2022) to 19,694 (2025) — a 46% drop (Fortune, March 2026)
- 41,000 entertainment workers left LA from 2022-2024 (Fortune)
- Bloomberg (March 2026): Shooting days in LA down 16% in 2025, roughly 50% below 2017 peak
- "The sunny version of Detroit" — Michael Lynton, former Sony Pictures CEO

### Box Office Decline
- US/Canada box office: $8.6B in 2025, far below ~$12B peak in 2018 (Bloomberg)
- Inflation-adjusted box office receipts plunged 40% from 2019-2025 (ZeroHedge)
- Audience numbers cut in half compared to pre-pandemic
- 2026 Oscars viewership: 17.86M, down 9% from 2025's 19.69M (The Mirror/Hollywood Reporter)

### Oscar Nominations Signal Shift
- NOT A SINGLE one of the 10 Best Picture nominees at 2026 Oscars was produced on Hollywood soundstages (Fortune)
- All were filmed elsewhere: New York, Louisiana, UK, etc.

### Employment & Studios
- LA's share of US film/TV jobs dropped from 33% to 22% in 2 years (Business Insider, Oct 2024)
- The "rest of the US" share rose from 54% to 69%
- 2023 writers' and actors' strikes shut down production for ~5 months
- Layoffs across Paramount, Warner Bros Discovery, CNN, and others
- Warner Bros Discovery sold to Paramount/Skydance's David Ellison, promising $6B+ in "synergies"

### Most Powerful Person in Entertainment
- Not a studio boss — Ted Sarandos, co-CEO of Netflix (headquartered in Silicon Valley, not LA)

## 2. Video Games: The New Entertainment King

### Revenue & Scale
- Video games = the biggest sector of entertainment, larger than film and recorded music combined (Variety)
- Gaming creator economy generated ~$10B in 2025 (IIDB, Feb 2026)
- Global gaming population: 3.5 billion by end of 2025 (SQ Magazine)
- YouTube + Twitch gaming content economy: $9.3B (OHEpic)
- Live streaming market: $62B in 2026 (Autofaceless)

### Platform Dominance
- Twitch: 31 million daily active viewers, 240M monthly active users (IIDB, OHEpic)
- YouTube Gaming: 100 billion watch hours, 40M active gaming channels
- Roblox: 85.3 million daily active users (end of 2024)
- Fortnite Travis Scott concert: 12+ million concurrent viewers

### Gaming as Social Platform
- Fortnite concerts, movie screenings, and interactive events blur gaming/social lines
- Roblox: user-generated content platform with 60B+ visits on top experiences
- Gaming platforms becoming "digital venues" — not just games

## 3. Gen Z: Gaming-First Generation

### Consumption Patterns
- 63% of Gen Z prefer playing video games to watching films (ESA 2024 report via SQ Magazine)
- 71% say gaming is a great way to socialize
- Gaming takes 25% of Gen Z leisure time — more than social media (18%) and streaming video (17%)
- 87% of Gen Z play video games at least weekly (Media Culture)
- 27% of Gen Z teens say gaming is their favorite entertainment activity (Deloitte)

### Platform Preferences
- 69% game on mobile, 42% on PC, 38% on console (SQ Magazine)
- 86% of Gen Z identify as "mobile gamers first" (vs 29% for Gen X)
- Average Gen Z gamer: 6 hours 10 minutes per day
- Mobile esports viewership surged 340% since 2022

### Content Discovery
- 73% of Gen Z prefer social media recommendations over traditional platforms (CTAM)
- YouTube, TikTok, Instagram dominate content discovery — not traditional media outlets

## 4. Streaming & Internet Entertainment Replacing Traditional Media

### Streaming Dominance
- OTT platforms (Netflix, Disney+, Prime) replacing traditional TV as primary medium (Coherent Market Insights, Jan 2026)
- People want control: on-demand, personalized, flexible viewing
- Cross-device consumption normalized (start on TV, finish on phone)
- Subscription fatigue growing but not reversing the trend

### Live Streaming Explosion
- Global live streaming: $62B market in 2026
- TikTok Live surpassed Twitch in Q1 2025 with 8B watch hours
- Kick emerged with 95/5 revenue split for creators (vs Twitch 50/50)
- YouTube holds 47% market share in live streaming

### Creator Economy
- 50+ million independent content creators globally
- Gaming is the single largest creator niche by revenue ($10B)
- Non-endemic brands (fast food, insurance, airlines) increasingly sponsoring gaming creators
- Authentic gameplay > traditional advertising for engagement

**Sources:**
- Coherent Market Insights - "Why Streaming Platforms Are Replacing Traditional TV" (Jan 2026)
- Autofaceless - "Live Streaming Statistics 2026: $62 Billion Market"
- IIDB - "Gaming Influencers" (Feb 2026)
- Deloitte - "2025 Media and Entertainment Outlook" (April 2025)

## 5. Gaming IP Flowing to Hollywood (Not the Other Way Around)

### Reverse IP Pipeline
- Super Mario Bros Movie: $1B+ global box office (Universal/Nintendo)
- The Last of Us: crucial HBO hit, joined "House of the Dragon" tier
- Half of top 10 most successful video game films released in last 5 years
- 50+ video game movies currently in development (Gamespot, March 2026)

### Gaming as IP Source
- Games have massive built-in audiences who spend hours in game worlds
- Franchise model from gaming > building new IP from scratch for Hollywood
- Even Roblox experiences (like "Adopt Me!" with 60B+ visits) represent IP opportunities
- Square Enix pivoting to multiplatform strategy, moving away from PlayStation exclusivity

### Distribution Disruption
- 70% of gamers play on multiple devices (Bain, 2024)
- Gamers identify with games, not devices (Call of Duty, Fortnite)
- Cloud gaming making consoles optional
- 8 of top 10 games playable on multiple platforms

**Sources:**
- Variety - "Gaming Is Hollywood's Next IP Frontier" (June 2023)
- Bain - "The Next Big Disruption in Video Games: Distribution" (2024)
- Gamespot - "50 Video Game Movies In The Works" (March 2026)

## 6. Entertainment Business Model Disruption

### The Old Model Dying
- Hollywood's "cluster effect" (talent, studios, infrastructure concentrated in LA) fragmenting
- Remote production, tax incentives, lower costs elsewhere draining LA
- AI threatening to reshape film production fundamentally
- Studio profits declining except select players (Warner Bros up, others down)

### New Models Emerging
- Creator-first: creators build cultural momentum, studios follow (Fox Creator Studios)
- Direct-to-audience monetization (subscriptions, tips, merchandise, sponsorships)
- Platform economics: 95/5 splits (Kick) forcing industry-wide revenue share changes
- Interactive/immersive experiences (Fortnite events, Roblox worlds) replacing passive viewing

### AI's Dual Role
- Could make studios more efficient while eroding content moats (Deloitte)
- Writers already leaving industry for AI consulting jobs
- AI threatens to automate production roles
