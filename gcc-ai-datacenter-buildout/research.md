# Research: AI Datacenter Buildout in GCC

## Overview

The Gulf Cooperation Council (GCC) — Saudi Arabia, UAE, Qatar, Kuwait, Oman, Bahrain — is undergoing a massive AI datacenter buildout, driven by sovereign wealth funds, Vision 2030/2035 national strategies, and partnerships with US hyperscalers. The GCC datacenter market was valued at **$3.48 billion in 2024** and is projected to reach **$9.49 billion by 2030** (CAGR 18.2%).

However, the US-Israel war on Iran (started early March 2026) has created significant disruption — drone strikes have already damaged Amazon facilities in UAE and Bahrain, and analysts predict the rapid buildout may halt for the rest of 2026.

## Country-by-Country Breakdown

### Saudi Arabia — The Leader

**Key Entity: HUMAIN**
- State-backed AI company under Public Investment Fund (PIF)
- CEO: Tareq Amin
- Secured **211 plots of land** across Saudi Arabia for datacenter expansion
- Strategy: "geographic diversity and multiple fiber-optic routes, leveraging the Kingdom's vast land mass"
- JV with stc subsidiary center3 to build **1GW of AI datacenter capacity**
- Secured **$1.2 billion financing** from National Infrastructure Fund (Jan 2026, WEF Davos)
- Partnership with **Qualcomm** for edge-to-cloud hybrid AI infrastructure
- Saudi Data and AI Authority target: **1.5GW total datacenter capacity by 2030**
- Datacenter market growing at **29% annually** (2024-2030)

**NEOM / Oxagon**
- DataVolt + NEOM signed **$5 billion agreement** (Feb 2025, LEAP 2025)
- Building **1.5GW AI datacenter campus** at Oxagon industrial zone
- First phase: **300MW** operational by 2028
- "Net-zero AI factory" — sustainable, data-driven economy
- Positioned as world's largest floating industrial complex

**Microsoft Saudi Arabia**
- Confirmed Saudi Arabia East datacenter region available **Q4 2026**
- Supports Vision 2030 digital/AI ambitions

### UAE — The Pioneer

**G42 + Microsoft Partnership**
- **200MW datacenter expansion** announced Nov 2025
- Part of **$15.2 billion total investment** in UAE
- Strengthening Microsoft Azure's secure, scalable, sovereign cloud
- Supports UAE's goal to double digital economy's GDP contribution over next decade

**MGX Fund**
- Emirati state-owned AI investment firm (est. 2024, Abu Dhabi)
- Target: **$100 billion AUM** for AI-driven investments
- Vehicle for AI infrastructure and advanced technology

**Broader Context**
- UAE is leading initial buildout based on advanced US partnerships
- Both UAE and Saudi Arabia lead based on their "advanced partnerships with US firms" (MEI report)

### Qatar — The Wealthy Entrant

**Qatar Investment Authority (QIA)**
- Partnered with **Blue Owl Capital** on **$3 billion datacenter venture** (Sep 2025)
- Sovereign wealth fund betting on AI infrastructure boom

**Qai (Qatar AI)**
- Launched Dec 2025
- Will develop, operate, and invest in AI infrastructure in Qatar and globally
- Delivering secure technologies across key sectors

**Current State**
- **13 operational datacenter facilities**, 5 upcoming projects
- Focus on energy-efficient AI systems
- AI strategy focused on data centres and data management capabilities

### Oman — The Strategic Bridge

**Omantel Transformation**
- Rebranding from telecom to Oman's national technology powerhouse
- Subsidiary **Oman Data Park**: Sultanate's largest datacenter operator
- Offers sovereign cloud, cybersecurity, and AI-as-a-service
- JV with Zain Group (ZOI): serves Meta, Google, AWS
- Operating **120,000 km submarine cable system**

**Infrastructure**
- Launched fibre network with Ciena for AI data needs (Nov 2025)
- **1.6 Tbps per wavelength** capacity (WaveLogic 6)
- Positioned as digital bridge linking Europe, Asia, Africa
- Supporting hyperscaler and enterprise cloud interconnects

### Kuwait — The Late Mover

**Ooredoo Kuwait**
- Launched nation's **first AI-enabled data center** (Nov 2025)
- Strategic partnership with NVIDIA

**Vision 2035**
- AI at the heart of national transformation
- Google Cloud partnership for infrastructure planning
- Microsoft MoU for digital transformation and AI training
- Minister: "Within next 10-12 months, we will see a decisive shift"

### Bahrain — The Cloud Pioneer Under Threat

**AWS Cloud Hub**
- Home to Amazon Web Services Middle East region
- Cloud-first strategy driving public sector modernization

**Iran War Impact (March 2026)**
- Amazon datacenters **targeted by Iranian drone strikes**
- 3 facilities damaged in UAE and Bahrain
- Iran cited Amazon's support for "U.S. military and intelligence activities"
- Cloud services disrupted, recovery ongoing
- Signals new kind of warfare where datacenters are strategic targets

## Power & Cooling Challenges

**Electricity Demand**
- Saudi Arabia targeting 1.5GW datacenter capacity by 2030
- NEOM/Oxagon alone: 1.5GW campus
- stc/Humain JV: 1GW
- Total Saudi planned: 3GW+ — significant grid impact

**Water Scarcity**
- GCC is among the most water-scarce regions globally
- Traditional evaporative cooling problematic
- Liquid immersion cooling gaining traction
- Google developing low-water alternatives (50% reduction)

**Green Energy Integration**
- NEOM DataVolt project positioned as "net-zero"
- Saudi Arabia aligning AI growth with green energy targets
- Abundant solar resources could power sustainable datacenters

## Iran War Impact (March 2026)

**The Conflict**
- US-Israel war on Iran began early March 2026
- Iran retaliated with drone strikes on commercial infrastructure

**Datacenter Attacks**
- Amazon datacenters in **UAE and Bahrain damaged by drones** (Mar 2-4, 2026)
- Iran targeted Amazon for "supporting U.S. military and intelligence"
- 3 facilities hit, cloud services disrupted

**Industry Impact**
- Motley Fool prediction: "region is likely to see its rapid AI build-out halt completely for the rest of 2026"
- Datacenters now recognized as "attractive targets" in geopolitical conflicts
- Fortune: "Iran's attacks signal a new kind of war as AI plays increasingly strategic role"
- Analysts warn hyperscalers may need to reconsider Middle East expansion
- Existing sunk costs (power contracts, land, fiber) make full relocation unlikely
- Low-latency requirements mean datacenters must stay near customers

**Semiconductor Supply Chain**
- CNBC: War threatens semiconductor and memory chip supply chains
- Energy price disruptions affecting production costs

## Strategic Context: The AI Stack Race

From MEI report "From Crude to Compute" (Dec 2025):
- GCC AI buildout is part of **US-China AI competition**
- US firms using GCC as "force multiplier for US AI capacity"
- Goal: outscale China's Digital Silk Road diffusion into Middle East
- UAE and Saudi Arabia lead with US partnerships
- Bahrain, Kuwait, Oman, Qatar should "plug into emerging infrastructure through coordinated planning"
