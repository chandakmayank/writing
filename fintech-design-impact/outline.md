# Article Outline: The Impact and Necessity of Great Design for Fintech Companies

## 1. Hook: The $900 Million Radio Button
- Citibank's catastrophic UX failure: $900M wired instead of $7.8M
- One wrong radio button, half a billion dollars lost
- Design in fintech isn't aesthetic preference—it's risk management

## 2. Introduction: Why Design Is Fintech's Most Undervalued Asset
- Fintech market: $280B (2025) → $1.38T (2034)
- 89% of users would switch banks for better UX
- Design ROI: $100 return for every $1 invested (Forrester)
- The dual challenge: B2C trust-building AND B2B complexity management

## 3. Fundamentals: How Fintech Design Differs from Everything Else
- The 50-millisecond trust judgment
- Regulatory pressure: KYC, AML, GDPR, PSD3, EAA compliance
- Psychological weight: Users aren't browsing—they're managing money
- Data density without spreadsheet fatigue
- High-stakes actions where "frictionless" can be dangerous

## 4. Strategy: Design Patterns That Build Trust (B2C Focus)
- Proof-first information architecture
- Friction-right onboarding with progressive KYC
- Fee transparency before commitment
- The three psychological pillars: Competence, Customer Orientation, Character
- Speed bumps for high-stakes decisions

## 5. Enterprise Reality: B2B Fintech Design Challenges
- The Citibank lesson and why enterprise UX still struggles
- Legacy systems, compliance hurdles, procurement priorities
- Design systems as governance frameworks
- TreasurUp/Rabobank case study: ironing out inconsistencies at scale
- Why "power users" still need great design

## 6. Accessibility: From Compliance to Competitive Advantage
- European Accessibility Act (June 2025) and its implications
- 3x higher unbanked rate for households with disabilities
- Inclusive features: screen readers, voice commands, keyboard navigation
- Tapping into underserved markets

## 7. The Business Case: ROI, Metrics, and Getting Started
- McKinsey: 32% higher revenue, 56% higher shareholder returns for design-led companies
- Key metrics: task completion, time-to-first-value, conversion rates
- Building the internal case: connecting UX KPIs to business outcomes
- Where to start: audit, quick wins, design system investment
