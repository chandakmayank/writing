# Research: Impact and Necessity of Great Design for Fintech Companies (B2B & B2C)

## Key Statistics and ROI Data

### Design ROI
- For every $1 spent on UX design, companies see an average return of $100 (Forrester Research) [^1]
- Companies prioritizing design have 32% higher revenue growth and 56% higher total return to shareholders than competitors (McKinsey) [^2]
- Design unicorns (businesses investing more in UX) see a 75% sales increase [^3]
- 89% of users would switch banks purely for a better user experience [^4]

### Fintech Market Context
- Global fintech market projected to grow from $280 billion in 2025 to $1.38 trillion by 2034 [^5]
- Fintech market growing at CAGR of 19.20% from 2024 to 2031 [^6]
- Philippines leads SEA in mobile fintech app adoption, increasing from 63% to 72% [^5]

### Trust & Psychology
- Users make split-second judgments about competence and trustworthiness in less than 50 milliseconds [^7]
- Interfaces adhering to strict grid layouts score 17% higher on perceived professionalism metrics [^7]
- Households with disabilities are 3 times more likely to be unbanked [^8]
- 24% of individuals with disabilities report facing challenges using online banking services [^8]
- 79% of customers report digital innovations in banking make financial services more accessible [^8]

## B2C Fintech Design Challenges

### Trust-First Architecture
- New users ask one question first: "Can I trust this?" [^9]
- Proof-first information architecture puts legitimacy cues before persuasion [^9]
- Users notice fraud, misuse, and opaque fees; if you bury proof, qualified visitors leave [^9]

### Onboarding & KYC
- KYC causes friction by design; UX must make each step feel purposeful and explained [^9]
- Show step count and time estimate before users begin [^9]
- Save progress automatically and support smooth re-entry [^9]
- Explain sensitive asks with simple, direct compliance microcopy [^9]
- Front-loading every compliance step before showing value is a common mistake [^9]

### Fee Transparency
- PSD3/PSR debates emphasize clearer fee disclosures [^9]
- Users punish surprises at confirmation with abandonment and distrust [^9]
- Show "You send / They receive" before the confirm step [^9]
- Include amount, recipient, ETA, and total fees on confirmation [^9]

### The Three Psychological Pillars of Trust
1. **Perceived Competence**: "Do they know what they're doing?" - Clean, consistent interface signals professionalism [^7]
2. **Customer Orientation**: "Do they care about me?" - Clear support options, transparent pricing, user control [^7]
3. **Character and Transparency**: "Can I trust them?" - Consistent behavior, honest communication [^7]

## B2B Fintech Design Challenges

### Enterprise Complexity
- Enterprise fintech platforms often run on older infrastructure tied to compliance [^10]
- Small UX updates can require long approval cycles in regulated environments [^10]
- Procurement values features over usability, creating feature-heavy but frustrating products [^10]
- Analysts and risk officers are treated as "power users" who can manage complex systems [^10]

### The Citibank $900 Million Lesson
- In August 2020, Citibank wired $900 million instead of $7.8 million interest payment [^10]
- Error traced to confusing interface design in internal payment system [^10]
- Difference between "sending interest payment" and "repaying full loan" was selecting wrong radio button [^10]
- Court ruled in favor of creditors; Citibank lost half a billion dollars [^10]
- Most expensive user interface failure in financial history [^10]

### B2B Design System Benefits
- TreasurUp (Rabobank) built design system that ironed out inconsistencies during rapid growth [^11]
- Design systems act as governance frameworks ensuring compliance consistency [^12]
- Reduce regulatory risk and allow faster rollout of new features without breaking UX patterns [^12]
- "Smart" components handle specific financial tasks: masking sensitive data, formatting multi-currency values [^13]

### Why Enterprise Fintech Still Struggles
1. Belief that UX slows projects down - creates larger problems over time [^10]
2. Regulatory hurdles and legacy systems [^10]
3. Procurement that values features over usability [^10]
4. The myth that experts don't need usability [^10]

## Design Patterns That Build Trust

### Proof-First Information Architecture
- Put value, one credibility cue, and one CTA above-the-fold [^9]
- Explain "How it works" in 3-5 plain, concrete steps [^9]
- Keep pricing and fees within 1-2 clicks everywhere [^9]
- Explain security in plain language with practical specifics [^9]

### Friction-Right Design
- In fintech, "frictionless" fails; friction-right wins with clear, timely explanations [^9]
- Design for intentional slowness when high-stakes actions are involved [^14]
- If user is about to wire $50,000 to unverified account, one-tap experience is a failure [^14]
- Build "speed bumps" - moments of forced reflection [^14]

### Progressive KYC
- Collect minimum data for activation first [^9]
- Stage KYC when users need it, not earlier [^9]
- Offer explore mode where regulations allow limited access [^9]

## Accessibility as Competitive Advantage

### Legal Requirements
- European Accessibility Act (EAA) took effect June 28, 2025 [^9]
- Covers banking and digital financial services across EU-facing products [^9]
- Accessibility now signals compliance readiness, not optional design maturity [^9]

### Inclusive Design Features
- Screen reader compatibility (VoiceOver, JAWS) [^8]
- Voice commands for navigation [^8]
- High contrast modes and scalable text [^8]
- Keyboard navigation support [^8]
- Clear error messages and recovery steps [^8]

### Business Case for Accessibility
- Embracing accessibility taps into diverse and underserved market [^8]
- Fosters more equitable financial landscape [^8]
- Inclusivity should not be an afterthought - must be inherent characteristic [^8]
- Creates "financial inclusion" gap when ignored [^15]

## Design Systems for Fintech

### Why Fintech Needs Specialized Design Systems
- Generic design systems don't handle financial-specific requirements [^13]
- Fintech design systems combine visual components with business logic and compliance rules [^13]
- Include "smart" components that mask sensitive data, format multi-currency values [^13]
- Ensure WCAG accessibility compliance automatically [^13]

### Benefits
- Ensure consistent, scalable UI across mobile and web products [^12]
- Especially important in fintech platforms with complex regulatory requirements [^12]
- Increase collaboration, ensure consistency, accelerate design and development cycles [^16]
- Modern fintech design systems act as governance frameworks [^12]

## Onboarding & Conversion Optimization

### Key Metrics
- Completion rate per step and total KYC drop-off rate [^9]
- Time-to-verified and time-to-first-value after signup [^9]
- Re-entry rate after abandonment and completion after re-entry [^9]

### Best Practices
- Validate inputs inline with specific, actionable error messages [^9]
- Provide recovery steps when verification fails or stalls [^9]
- Avoid vague errors like "invalid input" without guidance [^9]
- Remove re-entry points after abandonment is a common mistake [^9]
